Our Aims

The aim of DCER is to gain electoral reform in the UK. Where we differ from other campaigns is that we are working towards getting defined reforms with support from the broadest possible base.

Any political party's interest regarding electoral reform is not the same as the electorate's. Therefore they should not be exclusively entrusted to move the process forward without the clearest possible mandate of those with the interest, the electorate. Constituency parties collectively have a broad base, few interests conflicting with the electorate and influence over their parties. DCER will gather the support of constituency parties specifically to mandate Citizens' assemblies to decide on what the the reforms should be. The national partys will then have a democratic obligation to their memberships to include the reforms in their manifestos.

As well as reform of the Parliamentary electoral system, the popular election of a change of PM mid term and an elected second chamber are also supported by DCER.

Some notes & implications

It is also important that the differnt parties have the same policies for reform as differences would weaken messaging in the election. Differences would be exploited by the opposition as a sign of complexity and source of confusion.

Commonly held policies on electoral reform could form the basis for electoral alliance arrangements, meaning parties supporting the same reforms are more likely to succeed. The same reform policy would also assist the formation of a coalition if necessary.
